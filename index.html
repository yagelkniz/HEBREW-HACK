Greetings &
small talk.

שלוםshalomHello / Bye / Peaceall-purpose
בוקר טובboker tovGood morninguntil ~noon
לילה טובlaila tovGood nightbedtime only
מה נשמע?ma nishma?What's up?casual
מה קורה?ma kore?What's going on?street-level
הכל טובhakol tovAll gooddefault reply
תודהtodaThanksbasic
תודה רבהtoda rabaThanks a lotwarmer
בבקשהbevakashaPlease / You're welcomedouble duty
להתראותlehitra'otSee you laterformal-ish
Pro tip: "Ma nishma?" is rhetorical — like "How's it going?" in English. Just answer "hakol tov, toda" (all good, thanks). Don't actually launch into your week.

אפשר לקבל...?efshar lekabel...?Can I have...?universal opener
קפה הפוךkafe hafuch"Upside-down" coffee (latte)Israeli classic

צמחוניtzimḥoniVegetariancommon
טבעוניtiv'oniVegancommon in TLV
החשבון בבקשהha-ḥeshbon bevakashaThe bill, pleaseend of meal
כמה זה עולה?kama ze ole?How much?at the shuk
יקר מדיyakar midayToo expensivehaggle starter
תן לי הנחהten li hanaḥaGive me a discountexpected at shuk
יש לכם...?yesh lachem...?Do you have...?in shops
אני לוקח/תani lokeaḥ / lokaḥatI'll take itm / f
בכרטיסbe-kartisBy cardpaying
Pro tip: At the shuk, never accept the first price. A polite "yakar miday" with a smile will usually get you 10–20% off. It's the local sport.
